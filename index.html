The problem

Cyber risk is underwritten on inconsistent evidence.

Today

Security posture gets pieced together from self-attestations, point-in-time scans, and questionnaires that vary by broker and by submission. What looks like a strong control on paper may not be functioning at all. Signal is inconsistent, hard to compare across submissions, and stale by the time it reaches an underwriter.

With Pulse

Technical evidence is collected directly from an organization's environment, evaluated against control integrity criteria, and standardized into signals that map to how underwriters already think about risk — consistent across brokers, comparable across submissions, and current.

How Pulse works

From raw evidence to underwriting intelligence.

  1. 01

    Collect evidence

    Pulse gathers technical cybersecurity evidence directly from an organization's environment, rather than relying on self-reported answers.

  2. 02

    Evaluate control integrity

    Each control is assessed for whether it's actually functioning as intended, not just configured or reported as present.

  3. 03

    Generate standardized signals

    Findings are normalized into a consistent set of signals, so posture is comparable across organizations and stays current over time.

  4. 04

    Translate to underwriting intelligence

    Signals are mapped into underwriting-aligned intelligence that brokers and underwriters can use at submission, renewal, and across a portfolio.

Who it's for

Brokers

Organizations
